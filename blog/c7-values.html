My Values

When I think of the times of my life where I've been the happiest, proudest, or most satisfied, the following values come to mind, and on a scale of 1 to 5 on how much I try to live up to these values:

  • Accomplishment - 5
  • Amibition - 5
  • Autonomy - 5
  • Challenge - 4
  • Control - 4
  • Freedom - 4
  • Friendships - 4
  • Growth - 4
  • Honesty - 4
  • Hospitality - 2
  • Insight - 5
  • Introspection - 5
  • Knowledge - 5
  • Leadership - 2
  • Responsibility - 4

One value in particular that is important to me is being challenged. I like to learn in general, and the best way to do so is through adversity/challenge. Challenges motivate me to do my best, give me an opportunity to show what I am (or am not) capable of, and often teach me something either educational or something about myself. Challenges test the extent of my abilities and give me something to proud of after completing.

When I think about my values, and when I do something that reflects them, I feel very accomplished and elated. However the inverse is also very much true, if I do something that opposes my values, I feel a burdening sense of guilt and disappointment. I try my best to control the emotions I feel -- so if I make a cool accomplishment, I try not to get my spirits up way too high or get too cocky, and when I perform poorly, I try not to beat myself up too much, or else it would snowball into more failures. I do think that one can mediate stereotype if they recognize it by remembering their values and passion; one should understand him or herself moreso than others do and refer to their values when reflecting instead of believing in the negative stereotypes given to them. If I am feeling down, I will try to do an activity that would get my mind off of whatever is troubling me while boosting my confidence -- for example, playing a challenging but fun game is a great activity to increase confidence and ease stress. After some relaxation and breaks, I should be more than ready to tackle whatever is causing me trouble.
