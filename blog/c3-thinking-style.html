My Thinking Style and Learning

The biggest strugle for me in Phase 0 thus far has been the time management. Week1 was pretty straightforward, so I didn't have much difficulty, but Week 2, the material was more difficult, and I was faced with four midterms/quizzes in school simultaneously. I didn't manage my time properly and I ended up delaying my Week 2 material in order to study for school. As a result, the material carried over from week 2 to week 3 and made it a hell lot harder than it should have been. School was not as time-consuming, fortunately, so I had the opportunity to catch up. Another good thing for me was that the challenges this week were easy to me since they chiefly focused on Ruby programming, and I have prior programming experience. I did manage my time much better in week 3, but I want to continue managing my time properly so I don't have to deal with unnecessary stress.

Was I struggling because of the way I think I learn? Well, according to the Gregorc Thinking Style test, I am: Abstract Sequential (12 points), Concrete Sequential (8), Concrete Random (7) and Abstract Random (3).

  • Concrete means being capable of directly getting information, focusing on the present (practical), and not connecting the dots among different ideas/events/concepts etc.
  • Abstract is being able to conceive novel ideas, using intuition, imagination, etc.
  • Sequential means doing/thinking like a computer -- logically, and step by step
  • Random means not thinking in organized, methodical ways, but still achieving a goal
So by these definitions, I excel in the Sequential mindset, and I should be better at planning and working. When I get my hands dirty and actually begin working, I work brutally fast; I can quickly craft plans to tackle a big problem, be resourceful when I stumble upon a question that I don't understand, and for the most part stay focused. The reason why I struggled last week, I think, is that I had everything planned, but I didn't follow through 100%. For example, I hoped to finish my homework by Wednesday (and thus expected to do DevBootcamp stuff Friday), but I underestimated the workload and finished it Friday. Then I was burned out from that, and was incapable of completing the Bootcamp material. In other words, I made a big planning error which snowballed into chaos as I struggled to get caught up with my schedule. One mistake led to an array of other mistakes, and I believe that is why I didn't have such an easy time. And honestly, I did just have a TON of work to do.

When I plan properly, everything is nice and smooth--and even if I make minor errors, I can adjust and adapt fairly well. Now, when I make a major error, however, everything just falls apart. I do think the solution is pretty simple. I need to plan better and be more discplined. To plan better, I should pump some extra time in my schedule to cushion myself for when I take too long. So if I believe I can finish all my homework by Tuesday, I'll plan everything as if I finish Wednesday, so in the worst case scenario that I can't finish my homework by Tuesday, I won't be so ill-prepared. In regards to the discipline part, I need to begin things early, and if I act irresponsibly (starting late, not adhering to my schedule, etc), work hard until I get everything back on track, even if it means less time for gaming and sleeping.
