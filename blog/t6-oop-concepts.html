Class Methods

There are many concepts in object-oriented programming (OOP), and I will speak in length about one in particular: class method. Before I go into detail, it is important to have the basic idea of OOP. OOP is simply a programming paradigm in which data structures hold data and methods that allow one to manipulate that data. Most data manipulation relies on class methods, so it is therefore important to understand when and how to use them.

In OOP, objects contain data and methods. The data is composed of properties that describe the object, and methods provide ways to manipulate or access the object's data. They can also be used for "encapsulation" which is a fancy word for describing what information about objects are hidden and available to users. That may sound confusing, so let me provide an example.

In real life, you have probably driven a car. The car gives plenty of information to you, which is important when you are driving: your car's speed (so you don't exceed the speed limit), your car's driving mode (so you know if you are in park mode, etc), the radio station you are on, the sound indicating that your right/left blinker is on, and so on. The car also provides ways for you to change the car's state: you can change the radio station, use the pedals to change your car's speed, move the lever to turn on/off the blinker, and so on.

However, there are also A LOT of things going on in the car that you are not aware about and that you cannot change. For example, there is almost little to no information about how the engine of the car is performing, no information about the circuits and power being delivered, and so on. There are so many technical details about the car's engine (which I frankly can't describe too well) that is irrelevant to the person driving the car, and that is why it is HIDDEN from the driver -- or encapsulated. It is pointless for the driver to know. The driver only knows properties of the car that are important (speed, gas level, etc) and have the tools to change that (pedals, steering wheel). Likewise, in programming, there may be a lot of things going on under the hood that we don't want the end user to touch or know about, so we only provide him or her with what is relevant to them.

Class methods give users a way to interact with objects and can also provide information for the users if they request it. Class methods are important to OOP design because it allows programmers to encapsulate data as I mentioned earlier. You use class methods to determine the data that the user is aware of and can control and to fix the flow of data.
